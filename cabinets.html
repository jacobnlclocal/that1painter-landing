FAQs

Cabinet painting questions

Will the paint chip on cabinets?

Not when done right. Cabinet failures come from skipped prep (no degrease, no bonding primer) or wrong paint (wall paint on a cabinet). We use a 3-step system designed for cabinets - and it's covered for 3 years.

Can I keep using my kitchen?

Mostly. Doors and drawer fronts go to our shop. Boxes get sprayed on-site over 1–2 days. You'll have limited access to lowers during box spray days, but the kitchen is functional most evenings.

What's the difference between brushed and sprayed cabinets?

Brushed cabinets show brush marks and roller texture. Sprayed (HVLP) cabinets look like factory cabinets - perfectly smooth. Sprayed is the only way we do it.

Will it look as good as new cabinets?

Yes, with one honest caveat: if your doors are damaged (water swell, broken corners), paint won't fix structural issues. We'll tell you up front what paint will and won't disguise.

How much do painted cabinets save vs. replacement?

Painting your cabinets typically costs a small fraction of a full replacement - usually 70–80% less. You get the look of a new kitchen without ripping out boxes that are still structurally fine. We give you an exact price in writing after we walk your kitchen.
