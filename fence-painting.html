FAQs

Fence painting & staining questions

Should I stain or paint my fence?

Stain soaks in, keeps the wood look, and fades instead of peeling, so it's the low-maintenance choice for most Knoxville fences. Paint gives solid color and hides weathered wood but needs more upkeep over time. We'll walk you through both on-site.

Can you paint a fence that's already stained (or vice versa)?

Usually yes. Going from stain to paint or refreshing an old finish just changes the prep. We check what's on there now and prep it so the new finish bonds and lasts.

How long does a fence take?

Most residential fences are a 1–2 day job depending on length, height, and how much repair and washing the wood needs. We give you a firm timeline in the quote.

Do you finish both sides?

Yes when it's your fence to finish. Even coverage on both sides and the posts is what makes it look right and hold up. If a fence is shared, we'll confirm the boundary with you first.

Do you replace broken pickets or posts?

Minor picket and fastener fixes are usually included. Larger repairs or post replacement are quoted up front separately so there are no surprises.
